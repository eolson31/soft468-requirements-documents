# Interviews

We conducted three interviews for this project with Isabella Sanner, Michelle Hughes, and Lynne Henson. Details of each interview can be seen below:

### Interview 1

**Isabella Sanner** was the first person we interviewed. Isabella is a graduate student at UNL who is becoming a doctor of Audiology. She has conducted hearing tests with multiple people before and was able to provide us with the perspective of someone who would use the product being created. Isabella is also one of the sponsors for the Senior Design project. 

Key takeaways of our interview:

1. Current hearing test platforms require WiFi connectivity. This can restrict rural areas from receiving hearing tests.
2. Some current audio tests require manual data entry by the audiologist. A platform which can automatically record test data would significantly streamline the testing process.
3. Current audio tests have set frequencies which can be played. Increasing the number of frequencies could improve test results.
4. Hearing tests require specialized equipment (headphones, etc) for proper administration. These would also be needed for a mobile hearing test.

### Interview 2

**Michelle Hughes** was the second person we interviewed. Michelle is the Director of the Cochlear Implant Research lab at UNL and a professor in Audiology. She is also a sponsor of the Senior Design project. Michelle was able to share her extensive experience with hearing tests with us and gave unique insights to the current tools and their limitations.

Key takeaways of our interview:

1. Current hearing tests require specialized training for administration. An application which is easy to use, even by those who not have this specialized training, is ideal.
2. Collecting patient data, including demographic variables, is essential as it improves research results. 
3. Expanding accessability to hearing tests is the primary goal. Everyone deserves modern medicine, so an application which can push hearing test reach is desirable.

### Interview 3

**Lynne Henson** was the third individual we interviewed for this project. Lynne is someone we know personally and she has had hearing aids since childhood. This was a good opportunity for us to see what the receiving end of a hearing test was like and allowed us to see the reasons someone may not go for a followup.

Key takeaways of our interview:

1. Having to wait for test results can cause stress and anxiety. Quick test results are desirable for any hearing test application.
2. Lynne shared that she does a hearing test about every 5 years. She said the process for scheduling can be tedious, often requiring signoffs of a doctor, insurance, etc.
3. Currently, all hearing tests she has taken have been within an audiologist booth. This requires her to travel each time she needs a test. A mobile test would be more convenient as long as the results are just as good.
